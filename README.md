# Signals and Systems

## Course Overview

Signals and Systems is a fundamental course in Electrical, Electronics, Communication, and Computer Engineering. The subject deals with the representation, analysis, and processing of continuous-time and discrete-time signals and the behavior of systems that operate on these signals. It provides the mathematical foundation for advanced subjects such as Digital Signal Processing, Communication Systems, Control Systems, Image Processing, and Artificial Intelligence.

## UNIT–I: Introduction to Signals and Systems

Classification of signals, continuous-time and discrete-time signals, analog and digital signals, periodic and aperiodic signals, deterministic and random signals, energy and power signals, elementary signals, unit step function, unit impulse function, ramp and exponential signals, system concepts, linear and nonlinear systems, time-invariant and time-varying systems, causal and non-causal systems, stable and unstable systems.

## UNIT–II: Time Domain Analysis of Continuous-Time Systems

Linear Time-Invariant (LTI) systems, impulse response, convolution integral, properties of convolution, differential equation representation of systems, interconnection of systems, impulse response analysis, step response analysis, graphical interpretation of convolution, applications of LTI systems in engineering.

## UNIT–III: Fourier Analysis

Fourier series representation of periodic signals, trigonometric Fourier series, exponential Fourier series, Fourier transform, properties of Fourier transform, frequency domain representation of signals, spectrum analysis, Parseval’s theorem, energy spectral density, power spectral density, applications of Fourier analysis in communication systems.

## UNIT–IV: Laplace Transform and Continuous-Time System Analysis

Laplace transform, region of convergence, properties of Laplace transform, inverse Laplace transform, transfer function, poles and zeros, system realization, stability analysis using pole-zero plots, frequency response analysis, solution of differential equations using Laplace transform.

## UNIT–V: Z-Transform and Discrete-Time System Analysis

Discrete-time signals and systems, difference equations, Z-transform, region of convergence, properties of Z-transform, inverse Z-transform, transfer function of discrete-time systems, stability analysis, realization structures, frequency response of discrete-time systems, applications of Z-transform in digital signal processing.

## Course Outcomes

After completing this course, students will be able to:

1. Understand the characteristics and classification of signals and systems.
2. Analyze continuous-time and discrete-time systems using mathematical models.
3. Apply convolution techniques to determine system responses.
4. Analyze signals in the frequency domain using Fourier methods.
5. Solve system equations using Laplace and Z-transforms.
6. Evaluate stability and frequency response of engineering systems.
